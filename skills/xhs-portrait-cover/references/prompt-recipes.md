## Initial Cover

```text
Use case: ads-marketing
Asset type: Xiaohongshu cover, vertical 3:4
Input images:
- Image 1: portrait identity reference and main subject
- Image 2: composition/style reference only
- Images 3+: supporting logos

Create an original high-impact knowledge-creator cover inspired by the reference's hierarchy and depth.
Preserve the person's recognizable face, glasses, moles, hairstyle, expression, pose, clothing, and natural skin tone.
Keep the portrait's authentic background, slightly softened only if required for readability.

Headline, verbatim:
"[LINE 1]"
"[LINE 2]"

Small label, verbatim: "[LABEL]"

Use oversized bold display lettering with white fill, dark extrusion/drop shadow, and [BRAND COLOR] accents.
Place the portrait prominently in the lower half and use logos as small floating rounded-square cards.
Do not add Xiaohongshu UI, likes, username, view count, watermark, border, or extra copy.
```

## Restore the Original Background

```text
Change only the background behind the person.
Remove the artificial color-gradient or tech backdrop and restore the authentic setting from the supplied portrait.
Keep its original wall colors, practical lights, architectural details, and neutral ambience recognizable.
Soften it only enough to keep the headline readable.

Preserve the person's identity and every existing design element, including crop, title, label, logos, typography, positions, colors, and shadows.
Brand color must remain an accent only; do not tint the whole background.
```

## Add Frontal Face Light

```text
Change only the portrait lighting.
Simulate one large soft LED panel or softbox centered directly in front of the face, slightly above camera height.
Brighten the face evenly by about 0.8–1 photographic stop.
Lift the shadows beneath the glasses and around both eyes; add subtle broad rectangular catchlights.
Gently illuminate the front of the neck, collarbones, and upper chest for a physically consistent transition.
Keep natural skin texture, moles, facial structure, and true skin tone.

Do not add rim light, hair light, backlight, edge glow, halo, neon outline, or a cutout border.
Do not globally brighten or recolor the background.
Preserve identity, expression, pose, clothing, crop, background, text, logos, layout, and all graphic styling.
```

## Stronger Frontal Face Light

```text
Increase only the existing frontal face light by another 0.8 stop.
Create an unmistakable professional beauty-panel effect from the camera direction: strong, broad, soft, and even illumination across the forehead, cheeks, eyes, nose, lips, chin, jaw, neck, and upper chest.
Keep highlights controlled and retain realistic pores and facial contours.

Do not add a glowing edge, halo, backlight, white outline, oily shine, flat overexposure, or plastic smoothing.
Do not change the background, identity, text, logos, crop, or layout.
```

## Correct Accidental Rim Light

```text
The previous edit used the wrong lighting model.
Remove the artificial white/blue glowing outline around the hair, shoulders, arms, and clothing.
Replace it with a broad frontal softbox centered just above the camera, illuminating the face evenly and opening shadows behind the glasses.
The person must look lit from the front, not cut out with a luminous border.

Preserve every other element exactly.
```
